## 2026 年 6 月 · June 2026

这次把整套方法论从头到尾捋了一遍，订正 + 重写，核心是让它**更准、更好懂**。

*A full pass over the whole methodology — corrections and rewrites, all to make it more accurate and easier to follow.*

### 「学多久能到某个水平」——给了更实在的说法

- 把欧标（CEFR）各级别"大概要学多少小时"的参考表，换成了 **Cambridge（剑桥）官方**的数据
- 还补了句大实话：如果你不是零基础、本来就有学校英语底子，实际进步会比官方那个大数字**快不少**——你缺的不是从头学，是把学过的东西**用出来**

> **"How long does it actually take?" — a more honest answer**
> The "hours per CEFR level" reference table now uses **Cambridge's official** figures. Plus a reality check: if you're not starting from zero — you've already had years of school English — you'll progress **much faster** than those big numbers suggest. What you're missing isn't learning from scratch; it's **putting what you already know to use**.

### 「中到英怎么说成地道英文」重新讲清楚了

- 关键是**先别急着翻英文**：拿到一句复杂中文，先想清楚它到底要表达啥、用大白话讲明白；**最后一步**才翻成简单英文。很多人一开口就卡、说出来一股"翻译腔"，就是把"听懂意思"和"翻成英文"搅在一起了——现在拆成清清楚楚的两步
- **KISS 原则**（职场说话用简单短句就够、别硬堆复杂句）也讲透了它是怎么从前两步自然来的

> **Chinese → English: how to make it sound native, explained better**
> The key: **don't rush to translate**. Take a complex Chinese sentence, first work out what it's really trying to say and put it in plain words — **then, as the last step**, render it in simple English. Many people freeze up or sound "translated" because they blur "grasping the meaning" and "putting it into English" — now split into two clean steps. The **KISS principle** (at work, simple short sentences are plenty — don't force complex ones) is shown to follow naturally from those two steps.

### 「该练什么、时间怎么分」说明白了（Four Strands）

- 学英语其实要**四件事均衡着练**：① 大量听和读 ② 多开口、多输出 ③ 刻意记单词、固定搭配和语法 ④ 练"熟练度"（同样的话说得又快又顺）。这套就叫 **Four Strands**
- 这次讲清了**四件事怎么配比**，以及不同水平（刚入门 / 中等 / 高阶）分别该把劲儿往哪使

> **What to practice & how to split your time (Four Strands)**
> Learning English comes down to **balancing four things**: ① lots of listening & reading ② speaking & output ③ deliberately memorizing words, collocations, and grammar ④ building **fluency** (saying the same thing faster and smoother) — the **Four Strands**. This update clarifies **how to balance them**, and where **beginner / intermediate / advanced** learners should each put their effort.
